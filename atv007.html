can / can't

Can and can't have the same meaning as to be able to and to not be able to. They talk about ability. Can is a modal verb. It is followed by the infinitive without to.

could / couldn't

Could and couldn't are the past forms of can and can't.

Was / were

Was and were are the past forms of am, is and are. They are used with the past participle to make the past simple.

Was he at school yesterday?

Yes, he was / No, he wasn't

Were you at school yesterday?

Yes, I was / No, I wasn't

Were they at school yesterday?

Yes, they were / No, I weren't
